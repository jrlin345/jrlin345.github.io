Tactile E-skin Testbed

Designing and programming a modular testbed for tactile e-skin research

Overview

HAND-ERC is a Northwestern research center focused on the future of Robotic Hands. As part of the Summer Undergraduate Research Grand, which provided $4000 of funding, I helped lead the development of novel tactile sensors that rely on piezoelectric transducers and ultrasonic wave reading, through testbed development and designing new parts to integrate these sensors into hands.

As part of my project, I designed, programmed, and wired a modular testbed to test a new architecture of tactile sensors. I worked with Postdoctoral researchers on this project, collaborating on the design of the testbed and the calibration of the sensors, resulting in new insights into the performance of these sensors.

Research/Design Process

CAD of testbed

Step 1: Testbed Design and Manufacturing

I relied heavily on Onshape, Solidworks, and 3D printing to design and manufacture the testbed. Me and my advisors decided on 4 main parameters to test: horizontal and vertical distance from the transducers, distance from the transducers with a fixed probe, force applied to the membrane, and bend in the membrane. I designed the testbed with these parameters in mind, creating a modular design that could account for these different requirements.

Wiring and Sensors

Step 2: Programming and Electronics

I programmed the testbed with user-experience in mind, using Python to control the testing procedure and collect data. I created a modular codebase that allowed for easy adjustment for different proceudres and data collection. Additionally, I wired the testbed to interact with digital oscilloscopes, piezoelectric transducers, and F/T sensors to allow us to test all parameters needed. Wiring the testbed involved software-hardware integration, circuit design/testing, and signal filtering to ensure accurate data collection.

Waveform Analysis
Waveform Visualization

Step 3: Data Collection and Analysis

Over the course of 5 weeks, I used the testbed to collect data on the performance of the sensors, testing the 4 parameters we had identified. Ultimately we collected over 1000 data points with the testbed. I then analyzed this data to gain insights into the performance of the sensors. Using MATLAB and Python we did basic signal processing, data visualization, and statistical analysis to understand the performance of the sensors. Ultimately, our analysis found that the sensors needed much more calibration before it was ready for use in the hands, but the lab still uses the testbed to test new sensors.
