Underwater Treadmill

Designed for Geriatric Penguins at Shedd Aquarium

Overview

Shedd Aquarium was looking for safer and effective methods of exercise for older penguins. These penguins have joint pain and difficulty walking fast, so designing a mechanism that allows penguins to be comfortable exercising was a high priority.

So, me and a team of 4 designed HappiFeet! - a robust, primarily plastic, belt-powered underwater treadmill designed to aid geriatric penguins at Shedd Aquarium in exercise rehabilitation.

Continue to read more about our design process:

Design Process

Step 1: Client Meetings & Research

Throughout the design process, we interviewed the client to meet the facilities and to test our mockup designs to ensure they met their needs and expectations. Additionally, we researched various existing underwater treadmill designs and materials to be used in saltwater environments and around penguins, ideating multiple potential designs.

Step 2: Design

I helped manage the project using RAM and GANTT charts to create indepth deliverables. Additionally, it help lead design and manufacturing priorities, allowing us to fully manufacture and 3D design our prototype. We used tools such as Onshape, lathes, CNC routers, and 3D printers to fully manufacture our prototype and an extensive Bill of Materials to keep track of costs.

Step 3: Result

Our team efforts resulted in a final presentation, full technical/client final report, and fully functional prototype that was given to the client.

Result Details

Client: Shorefront Legacy Center

Date: March - June 2025

Our design features a fully plastic, waterproof treadmill that is powered by a 3D printed/wooden hand crank. The design also featuers polycarbonate dividers to limit penguin movement and a watertight acryllic tank.

Components:

  • UHMW Plastic Treadmill Frame
  • Hand-Crank Powered Timing Belt System
  • Soft, Yoga Mat Treadmill Treads
  • Polycarbonate Size Limitation Sheets
  • Water-tight Acrylic Tank
I personally gained more experience in CAD, team management, prototype manufacturing, and technical writing/communication
