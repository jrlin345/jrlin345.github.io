FlatTen: Modular Exhibit Display

Designed to display African American History around Evanston, IL

Overview

Shorefront Legacy Center was burdened by the heavy, difficult to set up, fragile display systems that they use to showcase black history around Chicago’s North Shore. They asked our team to design and create a lightweight, collapsible, and modular display system to display both 2-Dimensional pieces and 3-Dimensional objects. So, the purpose of our team’s design, the FlatTen, is to provide a less difficult and cumbersome system for Shorefront Legacy Center, while also remaining aesthetically pleasing and impactful in its messaging, and we created a prototype to meet these requirements.

1. User Research

Our project began with user research. We met with our client to disucss their needs and see existing products.

2. Manufacturing

I led the manufacturing efforts within our team, creating Bills of Materials, CAD designs, and using shop tools to manufacture our prototype

3. Results

We created a fully functioning prototype display for our client. The design features a fully machined aluminum frame, tension cables, and polycarbonate attachments.
Result Details

Client: Shorefront Legacy Center

Date: January - March 2025

Our design involves a collapsible system utilizing a frame built from telescoping poles. To hang media and display other objects, the frame will utilize tension cables and fasteners to hold things in place.
