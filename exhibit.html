Modular Exhibit Display

Designed to display African American history and increase user-functionality

Overview

Shorefront Legacy Center had heavy, difficult to set up, fragile display systems that they use to showcase black history around Chicago’s North Shore. So they asked our team of 4 to design and create a lightweight, collapsible, and modular display system to display different pieces of African-American History.

Our team’s designed the FlatTen display system, a collapsible display system that utilizes a frame built from telescoping poles and tension wires. The design combines lightweight parts, collapsibile features, and a aethetically pleasing design to create a display that is user-friendly.

Design Process

Client Meetings

Step 1: Client Meetings & Research

Our project began with user research. We met with our clients to learn their current problems and requirements. Additionally, we did market research to understand existing products and identify possible designs we can integrate into our product. All of this we documented in length in our reserach documentation.

Milling Parts for Prototype

Step 2: Design and Manufacturing

I led the manufacturing efforts within our team. This included creating Bills of Materials, CAD designs, and using the mill and 3D printer to manufacture our prototype. Additionally, we had to adapt our designs to fit manufacturing constraints, including adjusting to create 3D printed pole holders, something I lead and documented.

Final Prototype

Step 3: Result

We created multiple final deliverables including a poster, report, executive summary, and final prototype. We presented all of these at the DTC Design Fair where our prototype was highly received by our clients. I personally gained experience in manufacturing, team management, technical communication, and client interaction, all of which led to a successful project.

Final Design

Client: Shorefront Legacy Center

Date: January- March 2025

Our design involves a collapsible system utilizing a frame built from telescoping poles. To hang media and display other objects, the frame will utilize tension cables and fasteners to hold things in place. We also put together design documentation, including formal design reviews, testing reviews, final reports, and a final poster found here: Link

Components:

  • 7’ 3-component Aluminum Telescoping Poles
  • 36” Rectangular Aluminum poles
  • ⅛” Pulley Cable Rope
  • Cable Display Clamps
